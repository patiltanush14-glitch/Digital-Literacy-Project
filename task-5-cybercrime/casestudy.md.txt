Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common phishing attack happens step-by-step like this:
First, the attacker sends a fake email or message that looks like it is from a bank or popular service. Next, the message creates urgency, such as “Your account will be blocked.” It then includes a link to a fake website that looks real. When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures the information and uses it for fraud.

Students, working professionals, and elderly people are common targets because they may not always verify links or messages carefully.

The consequences can be serious, including loss of money, hacked accounts, and misuse of personal data. Victims may also face stress and difficulty recovering their accounts.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious online, avoiding unknown links, and verifying sources before sharing any personal information.
